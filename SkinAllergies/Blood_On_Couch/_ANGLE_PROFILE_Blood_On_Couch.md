# ANGLE 8: THE BLOOD ON THE COUCH

**Core psychology:** Visceral, graphic, inescapable physical evidence of suffering. The problem isn't abstract — it's on the furniture, the sheets, the dog's body.

**Awareness level:** Varies. What unifies them is severity, not sophistication. Some are early in the journey with a severe case. Some are deep into failed treatments. The common thread is the condition is visually horrific.

## Beliefs
- "This is not normal — something is seriously wrong"
- "I can't let anyone see my dog like this"
- "My house is a disaster because of this"

## Experiences (from research)
- Blood on couch, sheets, everywhere dog sits
- Paws described as looking like "hamburger meat"
- Dog scratching to the point of "pulling out hair and literally ripping skin open"
- Scabs, lesions, bald patches, crusty/pus-filled sores
- Paws "completely distorted" — permanent disfigurement
- Mopping and steam cleaning floors twice a day
- Washing dog's bed and all sheets/blankets daily
- Wrapping paws, putting socks on, taping cones that dog knocks off

## Emotions
Horror, helplessness, disgust mixed with heartbreak, overwhelm

## Behaviors
- Constant cleaning — blood, skin flakes, grease
- Wrapping paws, using cones, booties, baby pajamas
- Taking photos to show vets the severity
- Describing wounds in graphic detail when asking for help

## Specific desire
"I want the wounds to heal. I want the bleeding to stop. I want my house back."

## Key language from research
- "There is still blood all over my couch"
- "He is so uncomfortable that I can't even touch him without flinching"
- "Most days they look like hamburger meat"
- "Scratching to the point of pulling out her hair and literally ripping her skin open and bleeding"
- "Covered with big scaly bumps literally everywhere"
- "The bumps are so deep it's peeling his skin off"
- "She's covered... holes... raw skin"
- "I mop and steam the floors and carpets in my house twice a day"
