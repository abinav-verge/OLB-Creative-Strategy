# ANGLE 12: THE KANGAROO TRAP / DIET MAZE

**Core psychology:** Exhaustion from increasingly extreme dietary experiments. They've become a private chef cooking exotic proteins and their dog is still itching.

**Awareness level:** Solution-aware to product-aware. Deep into the food allergy hypothesis. May not have tried prescription medications yet, or may be doing both. Focused heavily on diet as the lever.

## Beliefs
- "It must be something in the food"
- "If I just find the right protein, this will stop"
- "Prescription food is a scam — $100 for a tiny bag"
- "My vet didn't explain this properly"

## Experiences (from research)
- Strict elimination diets lasting 6-8+ weeks with zero cheating
- Cooking "strict horse meat," rabbit, kangaroo, ostrich, venison at home
- Tried multiple commercial foods: grain-free, limited ingredient, single protein
- Spent months cycling through proteins — chicken, beef, lamb, salmon, one at a time
- Paying $100+ for small bags of Royal Canin Hypoallergenic or Hills z/d
- Dog hates the prescription food — "sleeping for most of the day," won't eat
- After all that effort, still itching — suggesting it was environmental all along
- 8-week trials where "no treats, nothing, it's a nightmare"

## Emotions
Frustration at wasted effort, exhaustion from meal prep, feeling like a bad owner if they break the diet, resentment at the cost of exotic foods

## Behaviors
- Meticulously tracking ingredients across every food and treat
- Cooking homemade meals regularly
- Cycling through increasingly exotic/expensive proteins
- Reading every label, eliminating one ingredient at a time
- Spending hours on food research

## Specific desire
"I want to stop being a full-time dog chef. Tell me it's not the food — or tell me what it actually IS."

## Key language from research
- "A proper trial is going to be expensive, because you need to feed him a completely novel protein he's never been exposed to before — reindeer, ostrich, or kangaroo"
- "I recently finished an elimination diet for Koda. He was on strict horse meat for 6 weeks"
- "The food is royal canin rabbit and potato"
- "I tried googling this but every recommendation is like $100 a bag"
- "I've tried different foods over the year including making my own and eliminating ingredients and still got 0 results"
- "We've done food trials and determined she isn't allergic to food — I thought chicken initially"
- "Over the years we have tried dietary changes and eliminating common 'hot foods' from his diet, none of which has seemed to make a difference"
