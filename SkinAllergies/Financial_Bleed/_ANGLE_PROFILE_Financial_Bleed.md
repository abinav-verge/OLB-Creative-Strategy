# ANGLE 4: THE FINANCIAL BLEED

**Core psychology:** Slow financial ruin with no end in sight. Not one big expense — a relentless monthly drain that compounds.

**Awareness level:** Most-aware to product-aware. These owners have been through the system. They know drug names, dosages, costs per pill. They're not uneducated — they're broke.

## Beliefs
- "The vet industry is profiting off my dog's suffering"
- "There has to be something cheaper that works"
- "I can't afford to love my dog at this price point"
- "Insurance won't cover any of this because it's pre-existing"

## Experiences (from research)
- Spending $130-$300+/month on Apoquel alone
- $240/month allergy shots, going up in price
- $460-$500+ per vet visit, at least once a month
- $100/bag for prescription food (3.5kg)
- Tried to get pet insurance — denied for pre-existing conditions
- Put themselves in debt for their dog
- Some paying for Cytopoint ($300/shot) every 4-6 weeks
- Allergy testing costs $600-$1400

## Emotions
Anger at the system, resentment, guilt about money vs. love trade-off, feeling trapped, financial anxiety

## Behaviors
- Shopping at Costco for cheaper Apoquel
- Searching for generic alternatives
- Comparing prices across pharmacies
- Calculating total annual spend
- Considering giving up because they literally cannot afford it
- Researching insurance options and discovering they're uninsurable

## Specific desire
"I need something that works AND doesn't bankrupt me."

## Key language from research
- "$130 per month. I can't afford that right now"
- "I'm so tired and broke from trying to figure out what's going on"
- "I've put myself in a lot of debt and this is my last attempt"
- "$300 per shot for something that barely works anymore"
- "Struggling to afford monthly allergy shot"
- "We love our boy so much but we honestly can't afford this much longer"
- "No pet insurance as no company will cover my itchy itchy dog anymore"
- "I feel like all of them are just trying to milk our hard earned money"
