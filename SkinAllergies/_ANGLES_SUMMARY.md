# SKIN ALLERGIES - ANGLES QUICK REFERENCE

Use this file to quickly identify which angle a piece of copy belongs to. For full psychographic profiles, read the individual `_ANGLE_PROFILE_[AngleName].md` files.

---

## ANGLE 1: THE 3 AM BREAKDOWN

**Core psychology:** Real-time desperation. Sleep deprivation. Emotional collapse happening right now.

**Specific desire:** "Make this stop tonight. Make us both able to sleep."

**Awareness level:** Problem-aware to solution-aware. Mid-crisis, not mid-research.

**Key indicators:** Posting at 3 AM, crying, exhausted, dog won't stop scratching tonight, both owner and dog can't sleep.

---

## ANGLE 2: THE INVISIBLE MONSTER

**Core psychology:** Fighting an enemy you can't see, name, or understand. The allergy has agency — it "wins," it "comes back," it "attacks."

**Specific desire:** "I want to know what this IS so I can actually fight it."

**Awareness level:** Problem-aware. They know their dog is suffering but don't know the root cause — food? Environmental? Both?

**Key indicators:** Years-long battle, no clear diagnosis, "allergic to everything," cycles between hope and defeat, adversarial language.

---

## ANGLE 3: THE GUILT + FAILING PARENT

**Core psychology:** Identity threat. The dog's suffering is evidence of their failure as a caregiver. External judgment from strangers confirms it.

**Specific desire:** "I want my dog to look healthy so people can see I'm a good owner." / "I want to stop feeling like I'm failing."

**Awareness level:** Varies. Unifying trait is they've internalized their dog's condition as their fault.

**Key indicators:** Shame, feeling judged by strangers, "horrible dog mom," crying, overcompensating with products.

---

## ANGLE 4: THE FINANCIAL BLEED

**Core psychology:** Slow financial ruin with no end in sight. Not one big expense — a relentless monthly drain that compounds.

**Specific desire:** "I need something that works AND doesn't bankrupt me."

**Awareness level:** Most-aware to product-aware. They know drug names, dosages, costs per pill. They're not uneducated — they're broke.

**Key indicators:** "$500/month," debt, calculating annual spend, shopping for cheaper alternatives, can't afford pet insurance.

---

## ANGLE 5: THE VET WHO GAVE UP

**Core psychology:** Authority abandonment. The experts have run out of answers. The person you trusted to have the solution doesn't have one.

**Specific desire:** "I need answers that the vet system couldn't give me."

**Awareness level:** Most-aware. Been through multiple vets, possibly dermatologists, multiple drug trials. Exhausted the conventional path.

**Key indicators:** Been through 3-5+ vets, conflicting advice, vets dismissed severity, basic prescriptions on repeat, trust shaken.

---

## ANGLE 6: THE MEDICATION HORROR STORY

**Core psychology:** Fear that the cure is slowly destroying their dog. Trading visible comfort for invisible damage.

**Specific desire:** "I want the itch relief WITHOUT the hidden long-term cost."

**Awareness level:** Product-aware to most-aware. Currently on medication or recently stopped. Noticed changes, read warnings, heard horror stories.

**Key indicators:** Fear of side effects, cancer concerns, immune shutdown, skin tags/cysts, aggression, lethargy, reading labels obsessively.

---

## ANGLE 7: THE WORKED-THEN-STOPPED CLIFF

**Core psychology:** The cruelty of tasting relief and having it taken away. Hope fulfilled then crushed is worse than never having hoped.

**Specific desire:** "I want something that doesn't stop working." / "I want to believe something can last."

**Awareness level:** Most-aware. They've found AND lost solutions. They know exactly what relief looks like because they had it.

**Key indicators:** "Worked for 6 weeks then stopped," increasing doses, honeymoon period then decline, fear of trying new things.

---

## ANGLE 8: THE BLOOD ON THE COUCH

**Core psychology:** Visceral, graphic, inescapable physical evidence of suffering. The problem isn't abstract — it's on the furniture, the sheets, the dog's body.

**Specific desire:** "I want the wounds to heal. I want the bleeding to stop. I want my house back."

**Awareness level:** Varies. Unified by severity, not sophistication. Condition is visually horrific.

**Key indicators:** Blood on couch/sheets, "hamburger meat" paws, open wounds, scabs, bald patches, daily cleaning/washing, graphic descriptions.

---

## ANGLE 9: THE SICK CHILD / LIFE CONSUMED

**Core psychology:** Total lifestyle hijack. Their daily routine, freedom, relationships, and mental bandwidth have been consumed by managing this condition.

**Specific desire:** "I want my life back. I want this to be simpler."

**Awareness level:** Varies. Defined by how much of their life the condition has consumed, not where they are on solution path.

**Key indicators:** Elaborate daily routines, "like having a sick child," canceled vacations, constant cleaning, resentment mixed with guilt.

---

## ANGLE 10: THE LAST ATTEMPT

**Core psychology:** Finality. One more try before surrender — whatever surrender means (rehoming, accepting permanent medication, emotional shutdown).

**Specific desire:** "Give me one reason to keep trying."

**Awareness level:** Most-aware. Been through the full journey. This is their last.

**Key indicators:** "Last attempt," significant debt, considered rehoming, "won't own this breed again," low expectations but still trying.

---

## ANGLE 11: THE ZOMBIE DOG

**Core psychology:** The medication killed the itch but also killed the dog's personality. They traded suffering for emptiness.

**Specific desire:** "I want my dog back — the playful one — without the itching."

**Awareness level:** Product-aware to most-aware. Currently on medication, seeing it "work" by one metric (less itching) while failing by another (dog is a shell).

**Key indicators:** Lethargic, personality change, aggression, obsessed with food, weight gain, mourning "old" dog, guilt about drugging.

---

## ANGLE 12: THE KANGAROO TRAP / DIET MAZE

**Core psychology:** Exhaustion from increasingly extreme dietary experiments. They've become a private chef cooking exotic proteins and their dog is still itching.

**Specific desire:** "I want to stop being a full-time dog chef. Tell me it's not the food — or tell me what it actually IS."

**Awareness level:** Solution-aware to product-aware. Deep into the food allergy hypothesis. Focused heavily on diet as the lever.

**Key indicators:** Elimination diets, cooking kangaroo/rabbit/ostrich, $100 prescription food bags, 8-week trials with zero cheating, still itching.

---

## ANGLE 13: THE DIY DISASTER

**Core psychology:** They tried to solve this the natural way first. Coconut oil, ACV, oatmeal baths, bee pollen, yogurt toppers. Their bathroom counter looks like a chemistry lab. None of it worked well enough.

**Specific desire:** "I want something natural that ACTUALLY works — not another bottle that sits on my counter doing nothing."

**Awareness level:** Solution-aware. Gravitated toward natural/holistic approaches. May not have tried prescriptions yet (by choice) or supplementing alongside.

**Key indicators:** Coconut oil, ACV, oatmeal baths, salmon oil, probiotics, Zesty Paws, elaborate routines, resistant to prescriptions, frustrated with wasted money.

## HOW TO USE THIS FILE

When you encounter a piece of copy (in the swipe file, from competitors, or when creating new ads):

1. **Read the copy and identify emotional/psychological triggers**
2. **Look for key indicators** that match one of these 13 angles
3. **Match to the specific desire** — which angle's desire does this copy speak to?
4. **If still unclear, read the full `_ANGLE_PROFILE_[AngleName].md` for deeper context**
5. **If it doesn't fit ANY existing angle, flag it as potentially new market segment**

Remember: **Angles are market segments with unique psychological profiles, not just "messaging themes."** Each angle represents a different customer with different beliefs, experiences, and emotional states.
