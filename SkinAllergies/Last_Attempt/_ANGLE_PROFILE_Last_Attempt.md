# ANGLE 10: THE LAST ATTEMPT

**Core psychology:** Finality. One more try before surrender — whatever surrender means (rehoming, accepting permanent medication, emotional shutdown).

**Awareness level:** Most-aware. They've been through the full journey. This isn't their first product, their third vet, or their fifth medication. It's their last.

## Beliefs
- "Nothing has worked so far — why would this?"
- "But I have to try one more time"
- "If this doesn't work, I don't know what I'll do"
- "I don't think I'll ever own this breed again"

## Experiences (from research)
- Put themselves in significant debt trying treatments
- Considered rehoming their dog
- Decided they'll never own this breed again due to trauma
- Been through Apoquel, Cytopoint, steroids, elimination diets, dermatologists — the full gauntlet
- Some have been told by others to consider whether continuing treatment is "fair to the dog"
- Years-long battle, not months

## Emotions
Desperation, exhaustion beyond tears, grim determination, bracing for disappointment

## Behaviors
- Framing purchases as "last chance" or "one more try"
- Low expectations but still spending
- Writing long, detailed posts cataloguing everything they've tried
- Emotionally preparing for the worst outcome

## Specific desire
"Give me one reason to keep trying."

## Key language from research
- "We almost gave up on our dog but we are trying one more time. I've put myself in a lot of debt and this is my last attempt before we have to give up on him"
- "I don't think I'll ever own one again simply because I don't want to see another dog suffer"
- "I was getting to the point where I was afraid I was going to have to rehome her"
- "Someone asked me if I thought she was suffering, and did I think it was fair to keep poking and prodding her"
- "I stopped the conversation there because I knew the direction it was headed"
- "She deserves a painfree life where she can run and play. I don't know what to do."
