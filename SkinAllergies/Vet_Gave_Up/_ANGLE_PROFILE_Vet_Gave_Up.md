# ANGLE 5: THE VET WHO GAVE UP

**Core psychology:** Authority abandonment. The experts have run out of answers. The person you trusted to have the solution doesn't have one.

**Awareness level:** Most-aware. These people have been through the full medical system — multiple vets, possibly dermatologists, multiple drug trials. They've exhausted the conventional path.

## Beliefs
- "If 5 vets can't figure it out, maybe nobody can"
- "Vets just keep prescribing the same things"
- "The vet isn't even trying anymore"
- "Nobody takes this seriously enough"

## Experiences (from research)
- Been through 3-5+ different vets
- Multiple vets gave conflicting advice
- Some vets dismissed the severity ("puppies are just itchy")
- Given basic ear cleaning solution for clearly infected ears
- Vet "basically gave up" and referred to specialist they can't afford
- Received the same prescription cycle: steroids → Apoquel → Cytopoint → repeat
- One vet couldn't hear a heart murmur another detected — trust shaken in competence

## Emotions
Betrayal, frustration, distrust, feeling dismissed, abandonment, "I'm on my own now"

## Behaviors
- Switching vets repeatedly
- Turning to Reddit/forums for answers vets couldn't provide
- Self-researching extensively
- Losing faith in the veterinary profession as a whole
- Some have stopped going to the vet out of frustration

## Specific desire
"I need answers that the vet system couldn't give me."

## Key language from research
- "Been through 5 different doctors and no one knows what to do anymore"
- "My vet just says 'puppies are extremely itchy'"
- "The vet didn't seem too sympathetic and didn't answer any of our questions"
- "I 100% third this — we had 4 vets say 'it's nothing, just give him Benadryl'"
- "Went to 3 different vet dermatologists... not 1 could tell me what's the issue"
- "The vet doesn't really seem to be bothered with just this particular issue"
- "Am a vet. That ear is infected. Go to a different vet if yours only gives you cleaning solution."
