# ANGLE 7: THE WORKED-THEN-STOPPED CLIFF

**Core psychology:** The cruelty of tasting relief and having it taken away. Hope fulfilled then crushed is worse than never having hoped.

**Awareness level:** Most-aware. They've found AND lost solutions. They know exactly what relief looks like because they had it.

## Beliefs
- "Nothing works forever"
- "My dog builds tolerance to everything"
- "I'm terrified to try something new because it'll just stop working too"
- "We're back to square one"

## Experiences (from research)
- Apoquel "worked wonderfully" then stopped after weeks/months
- Cytopoint "worked wonders" initially, now needs more frequent shots with less effect
- First Cytopoint injection was "miracle cure" — subsequent ones less and less effective
- Duration shrinking: lasted 6 weeks → 4 weeks → 2 weeks
- Cycled through multiple medications, each with a honeymoon period then decline
- Some had medications work for 2-3 years before suddenly failing

## Emotions
Grief, fear of hope, cynicism, feeling cursed, "what's the point of trying"

## Behaviors
- Reluctant to get excited about new solutions
- Describing timelines precisely — they track when things stopped working
- Comparing current state to "the good months" when medication worked
- Increasing dosages or stacking medications to chase fading results

## Specific desire
"I want something that doesn't stop working." / "I want to believe something can last."

## Key language from research
- "It worked wonderfully and she completely stopped itching. However, a month ago, her itching came back"
- "Worked brilliantly until this winter when it suddenly stopped being effective"
- "The first injection worked wonders and seemed like a miracle cure... but the effectiveness seems to have dwindled ever since"
- "They're $300 per shot for something that barely works anymore and I feel like we're back to square one"
- "After the first injection it was great... However she started getting bad again after ~two weeks"
- "Apoquel felt like a miracle... Apoquel stopped working 6 months later"
- "Cytopoint worked great at first, but then slowly faded in efficacy"
