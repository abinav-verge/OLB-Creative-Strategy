# ANGLE 11: THE ZOMBIE DOG

**Core psychology:** The medication killed the itch but also killed the dog's personality. They traded suffering for emptiness.

**Awareness level:** Product-aware to most-aware. Currently on medication and seeing it "work" by one metric (less itching) while failing by another (dog is a shell).

## Beliefs
- "The medication is working but my dog isn't himself"
- "I cured the skin but killed the spirit"
- "This isn't quality of life either"
- "There has to be a way to stop the itch without drugging him"

## Experiences (from research)
- Dog became "grumpier, more lethargic, less playful" on steroids
- "Obsessed about food" on steroids — personality warped
- Dog gained 5+ lbs in one month on steroid course
- Noticed "lethargy" on Apoquel
- Increased aggression as Apoquel side effect
- "Getting off steroids she was like a new dog — energy, playful, more stamina on walks, no panting. I felt bad for her having to deal with that for so long"
- Benadryl making dogs drowsy/sedated

## Emotions
Guilt about drugging their dog, mourning the "old" personality, conflicted — itching stopped but joy also stopped

## Behaviors
- Comparing "before medication" personality to current state
- Noticing behavioral changes and connecting them to medication
- Wanting to stop meds but terrified the itch returns
- Searching for alternatives that provide relief without sedation/personality change

## Specific desire
"I want my dog back — the playful one — without the itching."

## Key language from research
- "We do have concerns about the steroids — she was a bit grumpier, more lethargic, less playful and v obsessed about food"
- "Getting off steroids she was like a new dog! Energy, playful, more stamina on walks, no panting. I felt bad for her having to deal with that for so long"
- "My dog started having lethargy when she was on Apoquel"
- "He became more bitey on apoquel (increased aggression is a possible side effect)"
- "Rose is obviously not happy... she is now sleeping for most of the day"
