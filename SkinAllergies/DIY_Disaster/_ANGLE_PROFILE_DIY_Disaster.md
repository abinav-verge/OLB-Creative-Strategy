# ANGLE 13: THE DIY DISASTER

**Core psychology:** They tried to solve this the natural way first. Coconut oil, ACV, oatmeal baths, bee pollen, yogurt toppers. Their bathroom counter looks like a chemistry lab. None of it worked well enough.

**Awareness level:** Solution-aware. They know solutions exist but have been gravitating toward natural/holistic approaches. May not have tried prescriptions yet (by choice) or may be supplementing alongside them.

## Beliefs
- "I don't want to put my dog on drugs if I can avoid it"
- "Natural remedies should work if I find the right combination"
- "The pharmaceutical approach is treating symptoms, not causes"
- "There has to be a natural solution — I just haven't found the right one"

## Experiences (from research)
- Tried coconut oil (topical and oral), apple cider vinegar rinses/sprays
- Oatmeal baths, colloidal oatmeal conditioners
- Salmon oil, omega-3 supplements, fish oil
- Greek yogurt on food, probiotics, bee pollen (locally sourced)
- Betadine paw soaks, chlorhexidine shampoos
- Hot spot sprays, itch relief sprays, various medicated shampoos
- Benadryl, Zyrtec, Claritin (OTC antihistamines)
- Multiple supplements — Zesty Paws, quercetin, spirulina
- Some trying elaborate multi-step daily routines with 5-6 different products
- Results described as marginal at best — "helped a bit," "80-90% reduction" (rare success), mostly "didn't help"

## Emotions
Determination (fading), frustration at wasted money on products that don't work, overwhelm from too many options, starting to doubt the natural approach

## Behaviors
- Buying multiple supplements simultaneously
- Following elaborate daily/weekly routines
- Active in online forums asking for natural remedy recommendations
- Resistant to prescription medications but getting closer to caving
- Spending significant money across many small purchases that add up

## Specific desire
"I want something natural that ACTUALLY works — not another bottle that sits on my counter doing nothing."

## Key language from research
- "I've seen ads all over the internet for supplements to help with allergy symptoms but they're expensive and I don't want to waste my money on something that won't actually help"
- "I tried like.. so many of the supplements and creams for this issue"
- "Honestly there are really no supplements that are helpful for itch like this"
- "When it was bad I would spray a mixture of apple cider vinegar and water on him"
- "Her food toppers year round include organic ground beef... salmon oil... Greek yogurt... zesty paws allerimmune chew... locally sourced bee pollen... boiled beets..."
- "The only real difference I have seen is with the prescription medication and it is extremely expensive"
- "I like more natural, holistic methods and I've learned a ton about different products and practices that are supposed to help. Unfortunately, it's a long, slow journey"
