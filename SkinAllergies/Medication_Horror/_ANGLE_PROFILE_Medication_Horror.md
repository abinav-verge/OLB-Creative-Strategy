# ANGLE 6: THE MEDICATION HORROR STORY

**Core psychology:** Fear that the cure is slowly destroying their dog. Trading visible comfort for invisible damage.

**Awareness level:** Product-aware to most-aware. Currently on medication or recently stopped. They've read the warnings, noticed changes, or heard horror stories from other owners.

## Beliefs
- "These drugs shut down the immune system"
- "Long-term use causes tumors/cancer"
- "The vet didn't warn me about the risks"
- "I'm trading one problem for a worse one"

## Experiences (from research)
- Dog developed skin tags and cysts on Apoquel — $700 to remove
- Noticed increased aggression as a side effect of Apoquel
- Dog became lethargic on steroids — "grumpier, less playful, obsessed with food"
- Read that Apoquel "was never approved for long term use"
- Saw commercials that mention serious side effects
- Suspect Cytopoint played a role in their dog's cancer
- Dog developed UTI after starting Apoquel
- Weight gain on steroids (5+ lbs in one month)

## Emotions
Fear, anxiety, guilt about medicating, distrust of pharma, hypervigilance about side effects

## Behaviors
- Reading drug warning labels and forums obsessively
- Monitoring dog for personality or physical changes
- Wanting to stop medication but terrified of the itch returning
- Searching for "natural alternatives to Apoquel"
- Second-guessing their vet's recommendations

## Specific desire
"I want the itch relief WITHOUT the hidden long-term cost."

## Key language from research
- "Was never approved for long term use and might be dangerous to their health"
- "These two things both shut down the animals immune system and can cause a serious injury or death"
- "My dog was on Apoquel first — seemed to work but then she started getting skin tags and a huge cyst"
- "Getting off steroids she was like a new dog! I felt bad for her having to deal with that"
- "My dog started having lethargy when she was on Apoquel"
- "He became more bitey on apoquel (increased aggression is a possible side effect)"
- "I kind of suspect that Cytopoint played a role in my dog's cancer"
