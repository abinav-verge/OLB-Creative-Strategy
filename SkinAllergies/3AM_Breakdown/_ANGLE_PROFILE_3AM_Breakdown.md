# ANGLE 1: THE 3 AM BREAKDOWN

**Core psychology:** Real-time desperation. Sleep deprivation. Emotional collapse happening right now.

**Awareness level:** Problem-aware to solution-aware. They may or may not have tried prescriptions — the defining feature is that whatever they have isn't working tonight. Some are on Apoquel that "can't be given until morning." Others haven't found anything yet. The common thread is they're mid-crisis, not mid-research.

## Beliefs
- "I've tried everything and nothing is working right now"
- "Nobody understands how bad this is"
- "I'm failing my dog"
- "There has to be something I haven't found yet"

## Experiences (from research)
- Dog is non-stop itching, especially at night
- Owner wakes up multiple times from scratching/licking sounds
- Currently on medications that aren't providing enough relief tonight
- Has likely tried multiple interventions already but this is a flare-up moment
- Writing desperate posts at 3 AM to strangers on the internet

## Emotions
Despair, exhaustion, isolation, helplessness, frustration directed at themselves ("I feel so lost"), crying

## Behaviors
- Posting on Reddit/forums in the middle of the night
- Can't sleep — both owner and dog awake
- Searching for anything that could help immediately
- Emotionally reactive, not analytically shopping

## Specific desire
"Make this stop tonight. Make us both able to sleep."

## Key language from research
- "It's literally 3 am and I'm crying writing this"
- "We are both miserable"
- "She is non stop itching especially tonight and I can't give her more meds until the morning"
- "I just feel so lost and frustrated"
- "Wakes us up multiple times during the night"
