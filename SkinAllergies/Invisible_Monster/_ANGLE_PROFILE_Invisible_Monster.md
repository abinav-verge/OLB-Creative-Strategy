# ANGLE 2: THE INVISIBLE MONSTER

**Core psychology:** Fighting an enemy you can't see, name, or understand. The allergy has agency — it "wins," it "comes back," it "attacks."

**Awareness level:** Problem-aware. They know their dog is suffering but often don't know the root cause — is it food? Environmental? Both? The invisibility of the trigger IS the pain point.

## Beliefs
- "Something is wrong but nobody can tell me what"
- "We keep treating symptoms but the real problem keeps coming back"
- "Every few months it wins again"
- "I don't know if it's because of his age, but it's worse than ever"

## Experiences (from research)
- Have been through multiple vets without a clear diagnosis
- Condition cycles — improves, then flares unpredictably
- Tried both food changes and medications without identifying the actual trigger
- Allergy tests sometimes come back "allergic to everything" which is functionally useless
- Years-long battle with no clear enemy identified

## Emotions
Powerlessness, confusion, exhaustion from fighting something undefined, grief

## Behaviors
- Cycles between hope and defeat every few months
- Tries new things without a clear framework for why
- Describes the allergy in adversarial terms — "battle," "monster," "winning"

## Specific desire
"I want to know what this IS so I can actually fight it."

## Key language from research
- "It hurts to try and battle this invisible monster that keeps winning every few months"
- "I don't know if they are food or environmental or both"
- "No one knows what to do anymore"
- "She's allergic to everything it seems"
- "I did an allergy test, and my dog is literally allergic to everything"
- "I have no idea what to feed him"
- "They have absolutely no idea what is wrong with her"
