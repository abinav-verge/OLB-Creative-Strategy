## Real Customer Language

- **Incongruence:** "It looks like I'm... heavier than I actually am." They feel lied to by their own face.
- **Social Anxiety:** "I feel so camera shy and insecure whenever someone takes a picture of me."
- **Identity Crisis:** "In the evening I finally look like myself." This implies they spend most of the day feeling like a stranger.
- **Desperation:** "Ya girl is desperate." They are willing to try "unhinged" or "chaotic" solutions (like slapping their face or using hemorrhoid cream) to fix the issue.

- "Puff ball" — Why this phrase matters: It captures the feeling of being soft, rounded, and lacking any "edge" or definition.
- "Snatch my face" — Why this phrase matters: Fashionable slang for high-def, sculpted facial features.
- "Moon face" — Why this phrase matters: Usually associated with high cortisol (Cushing's style), it describes a perfectly round, featureless face.
- "Carb face" — Why this phrase matters: The specific belief that bread/sugar directly migrates to the cheeks.
- "Water retention/Bloat" — Why this phrase matters: It frames the problem as "fixable" and "temporary" rather than permanent fat.
