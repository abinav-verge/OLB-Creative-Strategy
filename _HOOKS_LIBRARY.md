## THE HOOK REALITY CHECK

**For VIDEO ads:** Hook must give IMMEDIATE reason to keep watching. Decision to stay or scroll happens in 1-3 seconds.

**For WRITTEN posts:** Hook can be more gradual since people can skim and decide.

**Critical difference:** Video = captive attention needed NOW. Written = browsing mode.

---

## HOOK FRAMEWORK: WHAT MAKES IT WORK

A strong hook for video ads does ONE of these (or combines multiple):

### 1. IMMEDIATE RECOGNITION
Viewer instantly thinks "That's ME" or "That's MY problem"

**Pattern:** Specific pain point + specific context

**Examples:**
- "My dog stopped greeting me at the door after two months on Apoquel"
- "I spent over $2,400 last year on vet visits and allergy meds, and my dog _still_ ended up scratching himself raw every night"

### 2. PATTERN INTERRUPT
Contradiction, shocking stat, or unexpected statement that breaks the scroll

**Pattern:** Thing that should work + didn't (or opposite)

**Examples:**
- "Apoquel stopped the itching. It also stopped my dog from being... my dog."
- "I'm a vet. And I put my own dog on Apoquel." (authority doing the thing they know is problematic)

### 3. CURIOSITY GAP
Open loop that MUST be closed. Viewer needs to know "what happened next?"

**Pattern:** Setup with missing information that creates tension

**Examples:**
- "That's when I finally read the label." (what did it say?)
- "It's a trap, guys." (what's the trap?)

### 4. FEAR/URGENCY
Problem escalation that viewer is currently experiencing or afraid of

**Pattern:** Worst-case scenario or hidden danger revealed

**Examples:**
- "Monitor for tumors." (fear-inducing label quote)
- "The second you stop paying, the itch comes back 10 times worse"

### 5. AUTHORITY/PROOF
Credible claim that establishes speaker has solution

**Pattern:** Authority position + unexpected vulnerability or insider knowledge

**Examples:**
- "I'm a vet. And I put my own dog on Apoquel." (authority + personal stake)

## HOOK CATEGORIES BY ANGLE

### MEDICATION HORROR ANGLE

**Best hook psychology:** Fear + Recognition (viewer is on meds, noticing changes)

**Examples:**
- "My dog stopped greeting me at the door after two months on Apoquel. That's when I finally read the label."
- "Apoquel stopped the itching. It also stopped my dog from being... my dog."
- "If your dog's on Apoquel and you're noticing personality changes, you're not imagining it."

**Why these work:** Immediate recognition for current medication users, curiosity about what's wrong

---

## GOOD VS BAD HOOK EXAMPLES

### ❌ BAD HOOK (No reason to keep watching):
"Apoquel finally stopped my dog's scratching. For the first time in months, no blood on the couch."

**Why it fails:**
- Sounds like a success story, no curiosity gap
- Doesn't relate to viewer's current problem
- No pattern interrupt
- Could be any random testimonial

---

### ✅ GOOD HOOK (Immediate engagement):
"My dog stopped greeting me at the door after two months on Apoquel. That's when I finally read the label."

**Why it works:**
- Immediate recognition (anyone on Apoquel noticing changes)
- Curiosity gap (what did the label say?)
- Pattern interrupt (medication success → something went wrong)
- Viewer thinks: "Should I be reading MY label?"

## HOOK CHECKLIST (Before Finalizing Copy)

**For VIDEO ads, ask:**
- [ ] Does this give an immediate reason to keep watching?
- [ ] Would a viewer in this segment think "that's ME"?
- [ ] Is there a curiosity gap, pattern interrupt, or fear trigger?
- [ ] Or does it just sound like a random story that could be anyone?

**If you answer "random story," rewrite the hook.**
