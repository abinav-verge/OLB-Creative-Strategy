## WHAT WORKS (Winners)

### B001 CREATION PROCESS - COPYWRITING EXECUTION LEARNINGS (2026-02-09)

**Context:** First UGC copy for Vet Who Gave Up angle. Went through multiple iterations to learn proper execution.

**What worked:**

1. **Unified Speaker Psychology**
   - Defined specific vet: practicing vet whose own dog went through medication cycle
   - Dominant emotion: conflicted empathy (caught between training and personal experience)
   - Clear motivation: "Can't watch others suffer what I suffered"
   - Believable because: personal stake (own dog)
   - Result: Every line flows authentically from this psychology

2. **Event-Emotion Weaving**
   - Not just "the itching came back" but "the itching came back. I felt like I was back at square one."
   - Not just "watching her at 2am" but "watching her chew her paws raw at 2am, and I just... I felt so helpless"
   - Result: Creates emotional texture, viewer feels the journey

3. **Proper Pacing on Mechanism Explanation**
   - Collagen in skin → mortar metaphor → what it does → PAUSE
   - Collagen breaks down → PAUSE
   - Cracks form → explain microscopic → PAUSE
   - Allergens slip through → examples → PAUSE
   - Result: Viewer can internalize each concept before moving forward

4. **Apoquel Revelation Given Space**
   - Expanded from 1 sentence to 7 lines
   - Emotional setup ("made me angry") → revelation → emphasis → implications → connect to experience → metaphor
   - Result: The "holy shit" moment actually lands

5. **Real Research Language**
   - "finally found something that works" (from customer research)
   - "back to square one" (from customer research)
   - Result: Immediate recognition, feels authentic

**Final script: 347 words, tight execution, emotional journey intact**

### MEDICATION HORROR UGC TEST RUN (2026-02-10)

**Context:** Test run writing UGC copy for Medication Horror angle. Went through multiple iterations to learn UGC-specific execution requirements.

**What worked:**

1. **Grounding with Specifics**
   - Specific behavioral observations: "stopped greeting me at the door," "stopped chasing squirrels," "gained 6 pounds in a month"
   - Not generic: "she changed" but concrete: "just laid there all day"
   - Result: Makes the story real and relatable

2. **Event-Emotion Weaving (After Feedback)**
   - Initial draft listed events without emotional reactions
   - Fixed version: "She stopped greeting me at the door. Just... laid there. At first I thought maybe she was just tired. But this nagging worry started creeping in."
   - Result: Viewer feels the journey, not just reads timeline

3. **Tight Mechanism Explanation for UGC**
   - Long version (7+ lines): Too academic, loses viewer
   - Tight version (2-3 sentences): "Apoquel doesn't fix anything. It just turns off the itch signal in your dog's brain. The allergens are still getting in through microscopic cracks in the skin."
   - Result: Viewer understands without tuning out

4. **Strong Hook for Video**
   - Weak hook: "Apoquel finally stopped my dog's scratching" (random story, no reason to watch)
   - Strong hook: "My dog stopped greeting me at the door after two months on Apoquel. That's when I finally read the label."
   - Result: Immediate recognition + curiosity gap = reason to keep watching

**Key insight: UGC video ads need ~180-200 words max. Can't artificially shorten by cutting sentences. Must capture full emotional journey more efficiently.**

## WHAT DOESN'T WORK (Losers)

### B001 EARLY DRAFTS - WHAT FAILED

**What didn't work:**

1. **Generic Speaker Psychology**
   - Early draft: Just "a vet" with no specific backstory
   - Problem: No clear motivation, feels salesy and suspicious
   - Learning: Need specific person with specific catalyst (own dog going through it)

2. **Event-Only Timeline**
   - Early draft: Listed what happened without emotional reactions
   - Problem: Reads like dry Wikipedia article, no emotional connection
   - Learning: Must weave emotions with events throughout

3. **Rapid-Fire Mechanism Explanation**
   - Early draft: "Skin needs collagen. Breaks down. Cracks form. Allergens get through. Immune response triggered."
   - Problem: 5 facts back-to-back, viewer can't internalize any of them
   - Learning: Each fact needs room to breathe, relatable connection, then PAUSE

4. **Apoquel Revelation Rushed**
   - Early draft: One sentence - "Apoquel doesn't fix cracks, just turns off itch signal"
   - Problem: Biggest revelation gets no room to land
   - Learning: Big moments need 5-7 lines minimum with emotional setup and multiple angles

5. **Performative/Cliché Language**
   - Early draft: "But I lost my dog" and "spark in their eyes"
   - Problem: Too dramatic, not how real people speak
   - Learning: Use specific behavioral descriptions instead

6. **Made-Up Language Patterns**
   - Early draft: Wrote how I THOUGHT people talk instead of using research
   - Problem: Feels generic, lacks authenticity
   - Learning: Always grep research first, use THEIR exact words

7. **Vet Using Uncertain Language**
   - Early draft: "Maybe we just need to adjust the dose"
   - Problem: Vets don't say "maybe" about their own standard protocol, breaks character
   - Learning: Speaker authenticity - language must match psychological reality

### MEDICATION HORROR UGC TEST RUN - WHAT FAILED (2026-02-10)

**What didn't work:**

1. **Event-Only Timeline (Repeated Mistake)**
   - Initial draft: "She stopped greeting me at the door. Stopped chasing squirrels. Gained 6 pounds."
   - Problem: Listed events without emotional reactions, reads like Wikipedia
   - Learning: **Even when principle is documented, I missed it.** Need examples + checklist in MEMORY.md to execute consistently.

2. **Weak Hook for Video**
   - Initial hook: "Apoquel finally stopped my dog's scratching. For the first time in months, no blood on the couch."
   - Problem: Sounds like random success story, no reason for viewer to keep watching
   - Learning: Video hooks need immediate recognition, curiosity gap, or pattern interrupt. Not just "starting a story."

3. **Copy Too Long for UGC Format**
   - Initial draft: 450+ words
   - Problem: UGC videos are 60-90 seconds, can't deliver 450 words naturally
   - Learning: UGC = 180-200 words max. Don't artificially shorten by cutting sentences awkwardly. Rewrite to capture essence efficiently.

4. **Over-Explaining Mechanism**
   - Initial draft: 7+ lines explaining how Apoquel works
   - Problem: Too academic for video format, viewer tunes out
   - Learning: For UGC, mechanism explanation = 2-3 punchy sentences max. "It's a trap, guys" beats academic breakdown.

## STRATEGIC INSIGHTS

- **Simplicity Wins:** Even in sophisticated markets (like anti-dandruff shampoo), simple execution can be more effective than complex logic. Don't over-engineer the "why" if a simple "it works" message lands (Spencer learning).
- **Direct Product Introduction:** The "Nic vs Flow" ad (Evolve) showed that bringing up the product very early/directly can work, even if it feels counter-intuitive to typical "delayed gratification" copywriting.
- **Complexity vs. Results:** "Keep it simple, you don't need some crazy logic." Focus on clear communication over elaborate psychological architecture when appropriate.
